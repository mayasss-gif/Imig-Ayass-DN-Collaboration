The Gap We Fill

Eicosanoid-DN research includes both observational and experimental work — GWAS loci, animal models, and mechanistic studies. But BiRAGAS elevates this further by unifying all evidence streams into a single causal framework: MR validation, do-calculus intervention modeling, hallucination-shielded QC, and cross-modal CRISPR combination predictions — systematically distinguishing true causal drivers from correlational bystanders across the full eicosanoid network.

The Non-Coding RNA Opportunity

No published study has mapped ncRNA regulation of eicosanoid genes in kidney. We can be first.

1,869 lncRNAs

From actual patient urine RNA-seq. Types: lincRNA, antisense, sense overlapping, processed transcripts. Ready for correlation with CYP2C, EPHX2, CYP4A expression.

382 Small RNAs

miRNAs, snoRNAs, snRNAs from urine. Can target sEH (EPHX2) 3'UTR, CYP2C8 mRNA, CYP4A11 — may explain inter-patient variation in EET levels.

ceRNA Networks

Pipeline ready (run_cerna_pipeline.py). Maps lncRNA↔miRNA↔mRNA circuits. Pearson r>0.4, ≥3 shared miRNAs. Discovers regulatory mechanisms.

Why This Is Entirely Novel

Zero publications exist on lncRNA → CYP/sEH regulation in kidney. Zero on miRNA targeting of eicosanoid enzymes in DN. This collaboration would produce the first-in-field discoveries in non-coding RNA regulation of fatty acid metabolism in diabetic kidney disease.
